Copyleft

You can read this article from the GNU website for more information. But in short is a licensing concept that allows you to do whatever tf you want with a work as long as you redistribute it with the same license.

Thus, this entire website is under the CC BY-SA 4.0 license (Creative Commons attribution-sharealike 4.0), the content (blogposts, pages etc) is under the CC BY-ND 4.0 license (Creative Commons attribution-noderivatives) unless it's explicily stated otherwise.
However, the javascript is licensed under the AGPL Affero General Public License. Why? You may ask, well, I couldn't find the magnent links for the CC license and make it so the LibreJS detected it as free javascript, so after some testing I managed to make it work with the AGPL (keep in mind that atm I'm still a novice). I don't mind since it's a free software license after all, but if it doesn't make sense from a legal perspective, please contact me to solve it.

Why these license? You may be asking. Well, I ain't no lawyer but after some investigation, I think it's best to apply a copyleft license to the website itself and a media license to the content respectively.
